My Journey of Bilingualism in Education

“Bilingualism” is often used to describe people who can use two languages fluently. My teaching experience and my own experience of being a bilingual learner make me have passion for exploring bilingual learners’ language and literacy development especially for those bilingual learners whose parents immigrated to another country.

Research indicates that people who speak two or more languages significantly outperform monolinguals in certain mental tasks, such as maintaining focus on important information.

Teaching academic content in two languages can be very expensive, and the prices of bilingual education programs are sky-high for most students and families. This is one reason why this education program model in its more intensive forms is typically limited to private schools.

  • 1) What factors do impact bilingual learners’ language and literacy development? I am especially interested in the impact of the home/school environment.
  • 2) How can bilingual learners’ parents and teachers support their language and literacy development?
  • 3) What factors do impact bilingual learners’ willingness to use their heritage language and to inherit their heritage culture?
